# Rasna — Project Brief (for new Claude.ai chat/project)

## What this is

I'm building a small tourism business called **Rasna** (named after the Etruscans' own name for themselves — fitting, since Blera has Etruscan ruins, rock-cut tombs, and the Via Clodia). The idea: bring small international groups (6-10 people) to **Blera**, a small town in northern Lazio (Tuscia/Viterbo area, ~80km north of Rome) where I grew up, for an authentic "live like a local" experience — not sightseeing, but real participation in local life: olive picking, traditional cooking, artisan crafts, etc.

## My partners and key context

- **Maria Grazia** — my local partner. She has deep relationships in Blera; I bring international audience know-how, marketing, and structure. We're co-founders.
- **Cristina Damiani** — being considered as a manager/operations coordinator (role still to be defined).

## Core philosophy (important — guides every decision)

**Low volume, high value, protect authenticity.** We deliberately do NOT want Blera to become "the next Positano." That means: small groups, a capped number of trips/year (~15-20 at maturity), premium pricing (€1,400/guest all-inclusive), and activities that are genuinely real — not staged for tourists.

## Where things stand right now (this is the live, evolving reality — not a fixed plan)

We started by mapping a theoretical 12-month agricultural calendar (olives, tomatoes, hazelnuts, grapes, Christmas, artichokes, asparagus, etc.), but **Maria Grazia gave crucial reality-check feedback**: many traditional activities (vendemmia, hand-wheat-harvesting, pig butchering, finding a "real Nonna") are increasingly rare or impractical. So we pivoted to building from **what's concretely confirmed**, validating person-by-person rather than designing a calendar first and staffing it later.

**Confirmed/current starting building blocks:**
1. Olive harvest — reliable, in-season agricultural anchor
2. Tomato/sauce-day (August) — confirmed reliable (Maria Grazia would even plant tomatoes herself if needed)
3. Renzo — leatherworker; would do hands-on bag-making sessions, potentially **for free** (since guests would likely buy his goods after) — strong souvenir-experience tie-in (Americans on Rome tours always ask for Italian leather bags)
4. Emiliano (Miriam's son) — cured meats/affettati producer; to follow up on his interest and year-round availability
5. Costantino (cheesemaker) — **sadly passed away ~1 year ago**, no longer available; may need to find a replacement cheesemaker
6. Maneggio di Civitella Cesi — horseback riding, a newly identified complementary activity to assess

**Lodging options identified in/around Blera:** Da Beccone (hotel/restaurant), agriturismi (Crociani Quinto, L'Introvabile — Poggio al Sasso possibly closed), B&Bs (La Ripa, Elegante Loggetta, Casamatta, plus Antonella as a direct contact)

**Local festivals that align naturally with trips:** Corpus Domini (June), summer sagre (most weekends), Cantine Festival (2nd weekend of November — wine cellars open to the public)
