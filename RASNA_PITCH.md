# RASNA — Authentic Blera Experiences
## A Partnership Pitch for Maria Grazia

*"Rasna" was the Etruscans' own name for themselves — the people who first carved this land out of the tufa rock 2,800 years ago. Blera still carries their footprint: the tombs, the Via Clodia, the cliffs. This project carries their name forward.*

---

## 1. The Idea, in One Line

We bring small groups of international travelers to Blera to **live — not just visit — the authentic rhythm of a small Italian town**: picking olives, eating Nonna's panonto in the field, walking the Etruscan tombs, exploring Lago di Vico, Villa Lante, Tarquinia, Viterbo, the coast — guided entirely by people who actually live this life.

Not a bus tour. Not a checklist of sights. A few unforgettable days as a guest in someone's real life.

## 2. Why Now, Why Us

International travelers are tired of crowded, staged "authentic Italy" experiences (Tuscany, Cinque Terre, Amalfi). They're actively searching for the real thing — small towns, real families, real traditions — and they're willing to pay well for it, *if* someone trustworthy can open the door.

**That's exactly what the two of us can do together, and almost no one else can:**

Neither of us could do this alone. Together, it's a complete business.

## 3. What the Experience Looks Like

A **4-day, small-group (6–10 people) seasonal trip**, built around one signature activity per season:

- **Late summer** — Olive picking, and lunch cooked by Nonna in the field (panonto, the Italian "BBQ")
- **Autumn** — Wine harvest and cellar visits
- **Winter** — Italian Christmas traditions: food, family customs, rituals
- **Spring** — Planting season, traditional pig butchering and curing

**Plus, woven through every trip:**
- Guided walks to the Etruscan rock-cut tombs and the Via Clodia
- Day excursions to Lago di Vico, Villa Lante, Palazzo Farnese, Viterbo, Tarquinia, and the coast
- Lodging with partner B&Bs, meals with local families and at trusted local spots

**Everything is included** in one price — guests show up, and we take care of the rest.

## 4. Why This Won't Hurt Blera — It Will Protect It

This is the single most important principle behind the whole project, and it shapes every decision:

> **We are not building the next Positano. We are deliberately keeping this small.**

- Small groups only (6–10 people)
- A *capped* number of trips per year (roughly 15–20 at full scale — a ceiling we choose, not a limit we hit)
- Activities stay real — people doing what they actually do, not performing for tourists
- Because we limit *how often* we run trips, we can charge a fair, premium price — meaning **fewer visitors, but much better income** for everyone involved, including the families and partners who host

In other words: this approach makes Blera *more* valuable without making it *busier*.
