# Rasna — Pilot Experience
## Lean Cost Model | Group of 6 | 5 Days / 4 Nights

*This is the zero-fluff version. The goal of the pilot is to test the experience, lock in partner relationships, and get content (photos, testimonials) — not to make money. Run it at cost or just above. Friends and trusted contacts only.*

---

## Pilot Objectives

1. Confirm which activities actually work with a real group
2. Build trust with local partners (Renzo, Emiliano, grove owner, Pino)
3. Identify what to cut, improve, or reorder
4. Get photos, video, and honest written feedback for the website
5. Test the van logistics (pickup, daily driving, timing)

---

## Pre-Departure Checklist (Do This Before Anyone Gets on a Plane)

These are the things that will kill the pilot if you haven't confirmed them.

- [ ] **Grove confirmed** — owner has verbally agreed, dates locked, harvest window verified with them no more than 2 weeks before departure
- [ ] **Frantoio booked** — pressing appointment reserved (they book up in October)
- [ ] **Renzo available** — date confirmed, project scope agreed (see below on timing)
- [ ] **Emiliano confirmed or backup decided** — if not confirmed 3 weeks out, activate backup slot (horse riding or Norchia hike with Pino)
- [ ] **Antonella's B&B — 3 rooms reserved**, deposit paid, ZTL/parking situation clarified with Pino
- [ ] **Van booked** at FCO (Europcar/Hertz/Sixt), 9-seater, confirmed it fits 6 adults + bags
- [ ] **Activity insurance** — policy read, hands-on activities (harvest, leather) explicitly covered, exclusions understood
- [ ] **Guest dietary restrictions** collected in writing — not asked day-of
- [ ] **Cancellation policy sent and acknowledged** by all guests (50% non-refundable inside 30 days, 100% inside 7 days)
- [ ] **€850/person deposits received** before booking anything non-refundable
- [ ] **Weather check** — 5-day forecast reviewed 3 days before. Rain contingencies activated if needed (see Day 2 and Day 4)
- [ ] **Next group date decided** — set before the pilot departs. Do not leave this until after.

*Book at Europcar, Hertz, or Sixt at Fiumicino — all have 9-seaters. Book at least 3 weeks ahead in October. Confirm the vehicle fits 6 adults + luggage before you arrive.*

Budget €15 for paid parking across the week.

### Day 1 — Pickup & Settle In

| Time | Activity | Cost |
|------|----------|------|
| 12:30 | Pick up van at FCO | — |
| 13:00 | Meet guests at Arrivals | — |
| 13:30 | Drive north to Blera (~1h 20m) | Fuel included |
| 15:00 | Check in — Antonella's B&B | — |
| 15:30 | Welcome walk — piazza, church, ravine viewpoint | Free |
| 16:30 | Caffè and aperitivo | Bar Etruria — €40 |
| 19:30 | Welcome dinner | Beccone / La Torretta — €210 |

> **Contingency — flight delayed, guests arrive late afternoon:**
> Skip the welcome walk. Go straight to Bar Etruria for aperitivo (it can flex to 17:30–18:30). Push dinner to 20:00. The walk can happen Day 2 morning before the grove if needed — it's 30 minutes, works at any point.

> **Contingency — someone in the group is travel-sick or exhausted:**
> Keep Day 1 light. The walk is optional. If someone needs to rest at the B&B while others do the aperitivo, that's fine. Don't force group cohesion on Day 1 — it builds naturally.

### Day 2 — Olive Harvest & Panonto

| Time | Activity | Cost |
|------|----------|------|
| 07:30 | Self-catered breakfast at B&B | €35 |
| 08:00 | Drive to grove | — |
| 08:15 | **Harvest** — rake, sort, net. Midway break: bread, oil, water | €150 (grove) |
| 12:30 | Drive to frantoio | — |
| 12:45 | **Oil pressing** — watch, taste, bottle each | €130 (pressing + bottles) |
| 13:30 | **Panonto** — back at the grove. Light the brace. Salsicce, pane, olio, vino | €80 |
| 15:30 | Return to B&B | — |
| 17:00 | Walk to necropolis (optional) | Free |
| 19:30 | Dinner | Beccone — €180 |

> **Contingency — it rains on Day 2:**
> The harvest can happen in light rain — it's messy but authentic, and guests will remember it. Heavy rain: postpone harvest to Day 3 morning (swap with Renzo, who moves to Day 2 afternoon indoors). Panonto under rain doesn't work — move to a covered lunch at Beccone (€60 extra, simple menu). Flag this scenario to the grove owner in advance: "what do we do if it rains on our day?" Get their answer before the trip.

> **Contingency — olives not ready (harvest window off by a week or two):**
> This is the highest-probability risk of the whole trip. Confirm with the grove owner 2 weeks before AND 3 days before. If olives aren't ready: replace harvest with a full San Giovenale hike (Pino leads, half day, free), visit the frantoio anyway to see the machinery and taste last year's oil, and do the panonto as a standalone event in the evening with Pino's brace. It's not the same, but it's still a coherent day.

> **Contingency — someone doesn't want to do physical harvest work:**
> Don't push. They can help with sorting (seated), watch from the edge, or take photos. Assign them the "official photographer" role. Most people who say they're not up for it end up joining once they see the others — but don't make it awkward if they don't.

> **Note on Renzo's timing:** If the leather workshop is scheduled for Day 3 morning, brief Renzo on scope before the day. A card wallet takes 2h with 6 people; a belt takes 4h+. For the pilot, agree on a simple object — keyring or card wallet — so the session ends on time and the afternoon isn't lost.

| 09:00 | **Bulicame** — free natural thermal spring, Dante stop, 20 min | Free |
| 09:30 | **Viterbo on foot** — Quartiere San Pellegrino, 1.5h | Free |

| 13:00 | **Lunch** — Trattoria del Richiastro | €180 |

> **Day 4 is the most overloaded day.** Five stops. If anything runs over, the first thing to cut is **Bulicame** (interesting but not essential — 20-min stop, skip it if behind schedule). The second thing to cut is **Villa Lante** (exterior walk only on the pilot anyway). **Do not cut the lake** — it's the best 45 minutes of the day and costs almost nothing.

> **Contingency — it rains on Day 4:**
> Viterbo's San Pellegrino quarter works in rain — it's a medieval alley walk, umbrellas are fine, and it actually looks better in wet weather. The lake walk does not work in heavy rain. Replacement: skip the lake and instead visit the **Palazzo dei Papi** in Viterbo (interior, undercover, €4/person) or extend lunch and go straight back to Blera for an early, relaxed evening.

> **Contingency — Trattoria del Richiastro is closed or full:**
> Book the restaurant before the trip, not on the day. If unavailable: **Osteria del Vecchio Orologio** or another central Viterbo trattoria — ask Maria Grazia for the name of whoever she trusts in Viterbo. Always have a backup restaurant in the same city.

## Group Dynamics — What To Watch For

*Small groups amplify everything — good and bad. This is a 5-day trip in a van with 6 people.*

| Situation | What to do |
|-----------|-----------|
| One person dominates conversations with locals | Gently rotate who asks questions. Brief the group before Renzo and Emiliano: "let him explain first, then ask." |
| Two people in the group have tension | Don't take sides. Give everyone independent free time every afternoon — this is built into the schedule. |
| Someone is clearly not enjoying an activity | Acknowledge it privately, don't make it a group moment. Give them something useful to do — photos, carrying things, talking to the host. |
| The group is having a great moment | Stop. Don't narrate it or move on too quickly. Let it breathe. These are the moments that become testimonials. |
| Someone gets hurt (blisters, twisted ankle, minor cut at Renzo's) | First aid kit in the van. Know where the nearest pharmacy (farmacia) is in Blera. Hospital: Ospedale di Viterbo, ~25 min. |

## What to Document During the Pilot

*This is as important as the experience itself.*

- [ ] Photo of every activity (harvest, panonto fire, press, leather, affettati board, lake)
- [ ] Short video: oil coming out of the press, the brace being lit, Renzo's hands on the leather
- [ ] Note exact timings — when each session actually started and ended vs planned
- [ ] Note any moment where energy dropped or someone disengaged
- [ ] Note any moment where the group went quiet in a good way
- [ ] Written feedback from each guest within 48h of returning (5 questions max: what was the highlight? what would you cut? would you recommend it? what would you pay? one word for the trip)
- [ ] Confirm with each partner: do they want to do it again, and at what rate?
- [ ] Identify which activities were genuinely participatory vs which ones guests just watched

## After the Pilot — Do This Before Life Gets in the Way

| Action | Deadline |
|--------|----------|
| Debrief with Maria Grazia | Within 1 week |
| Send thank-you and feedback form to guests | Within 48 hours of return |
| Revise the itinerary based on what actually happened | Within 2 weeks |
| Lock the date for the next group | Already decided before the pilot — just confirm it |
| Start building the website or landing page | Within 1 month |

*The most common failure mode isn't a bad experience — it's losing momentum after the pilot. You come back, life happens, Maria Grazia gets busy, and the website never gets built. Set the next group date before anyone gets on the plane home.*
