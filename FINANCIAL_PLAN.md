Recommended starting price: **ÔéČ1,100/guest, all-inclusive** ÔÇö covers lodging, all meals (whether at partner restaurants or with local families), guiding/transport during the trip, and all activity/artisan fees. The guest's only extra cost is getting to/from Blera and personal spending (souvenirs, drinks beyond what's included, etc.). All-inclusive pricing is also a strong selling point for this type of niche travel ÔÇö guests don't want to do logistics, they want to show up and be taken care of.

**Note on licensing ÔÇö informal "Nonna's house" cooking classes:** this is the heart of the experience, but it's also exactly the kind of activity Italian food-safety/tourism rules are designed to regulate (cooking for paying foreign guests in a private home, repeatedly, as part of a commercial package). "Informal" works for a one-off favor between friends ÔÇö once you're charging guests as part of a paid product, it stops being informal in the eyes of the law, even if it feels casual on the day.

Two practical paths, to weigh with a local commercialista in Phase 4:
1. **Keep it genuinely informal and low-frequency** ÔÇö e.g., positioned/structured as a "meal with a local family" rather than a formal class, at a scale that stays under whatever thresholds Italian home-restaurant rules allow (Italy does have a registered framework for this ÔÇö *agriturismo* and *home restaurant / cesarine*-style setups ÔÇö worth researching whether Nonna could register under one of these).
2. **Channel it through an already-licensed venue or person** ÔÇö e.g., Nonna partners with or operates under a licensed agriturismo/restaurant's permit, or the "class" happens at a partner restaurant's kitchen with Nonna as the guest teacher. This keeps the authentic feel while putting the legal/compliance weight on an entity that already has it sorted.

Either way, this is a compliance risk worth resolving *before* scaling ÔÇö not something to leave informal indefinitely, since it's one of the activities most likely to draw scrutiny (food + paying tourists + a private home).
